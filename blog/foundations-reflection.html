How much I have built myself during my Dev Academy exploration

2nd October 2021

top 3 findings

How much I grow when I am vulnerable

At the start of this course, I promised myself that THIS, new, journey I was embarking on, was to better myself in every aspect. I feel I have done this, or at least, I think I have. My physical body is not in amazing shape. Lockdown sure does make me eat. But, I love it and I love myself.

Being able to express my own doubts has been a struggle for me. I was always intimidated when I felt looked down on but the core reflections allowed me to not feel singled out. It kept me embracing that I am part of something bigger than my own worries.

Wait... My brain can change?

Neuroplasticity has saved my life. Growth mindset has saved my life. Being able to feel like I am not a lost cause has saved my life. I am so thankful for the lessons on these topics. I have always thought that I could only have a finite amount of knowledge. How wrong I was.

Even if I am not able to understand or use everything I now learn I am not scared or intimidated because I don't know it, YET, but I will know it soon. Having that amount of confidence in my abilities has shaped me to be more accepting of challenges and obstacles that occur.

I can actually stick to a plan?!

Woah, New'r Me can do THIS?? Crazy. I have kept to my word of asking for help. Maybe not as often as I should be, but I still am. This has been a problem I have faced my entire life. Thinking I can attack the world on my own without support. Now that I have such an incredible support system. I know moving forward in my life is going to be a fun and exciting.

human skills / programming

Whats the point?

I honestly know that if we were NOT taught about these super important core human skills. I would be struggling, immensely. The topics we covered were life changing. It gave me a different way of thinking! It gave me hope that I can be better.

Students like me (super inexperienced in programming) would struggle to handle the amount of content sheer complexity of the subject. I found that I am able to control my stress level better knowing that I am getting better.

Why code without trying to be a better human?

All the time I spent researching the core skills, the more confident I felt in my ability to grow. If I only learnt programming. I feel like I would still struggle with a lot of personal issues. I would also feel very insecure. Not any more though.

The understanding of myself is far greater than learning JS fundamentals (still very important). I just now know how to handle these situations where I feel 'stupid' but are actually just growing new neural pathways.
